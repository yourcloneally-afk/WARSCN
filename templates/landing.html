HOW IT WORKS

Designed for people in conflict zones

Not for pundits. For civilians, aid workers, journalists, and families who need fast, accurate information to stay safe.

Live Intelligence Feed

Headlines from BBC, Al Jazeera, Reuters, Jerusalem Post, Times of Israel, Iran International, ISW, Bellingcat, Telegram OSINT channels and 15+ more — filtered to only Iran/Israel/conflict content and refreshed every 2 minutes.

  • Relevance-filtered — no noise
  • Confirmed / Developing / Rumored labels
  • Sorted by recency

Interactive Conflict Map

Every event is geocoded and plotted on a live Leaflet map. Circle size and colour indicate severity. Toggle a heatmap overlay or safe-zone markers (hospitals, embassies, shelters).

  • Red = critical, yellow = developing, blue = low
  • Click any dot for details
  • Heatmap & safe-zone overlays

Evacuation Routing

Enter your destination and EvacScan calculates a route, shows distance and estimated travel time, and draws it on the map. Routes avoid high-severity danger zones where possible.

  • Works with or without routing API
  • Turn-by-turn step list
  • Download as PDF

Proximity Alerts

Set your location and alert radius. EvacScan will show you events within that range. Optionally provide email or phone for out-of-browser notifications.

  • Configurable radius (10–500 km)
  • Optional email / SMS
  • No account required

I'm Safe Reports

Generate a shareable link that lets family and friends know you're safe. No login required. The link expires automatically after 24 hours for privacy.

  • One tap from the green button on map
  • Anonymous, token-based
  • Auto-expires after 24h

Emergency Resources

Emergency numbers for Iran, Israel, and internationally. Embassy contacts. First aid reference. Go-bag checklist. Available offline once loaded.

  • Iran, Israel, US numbers
  • UNHCR, ICRC, MSF contacts
  • Go-bag & first aid guide

QUICK GUIDE

How to use the dashboard

1

Read the Feed

The right panel shows the live event feed. Use the filter chips (Conflict / Strike / Confirmed / Developing) to narrow down. Click any card to jump to that location on the map.

2

Explore the Map

Each coloured circle is an event. Red = critical severity, Yellow = developing, Blue = lower severity. Click a dot for a headline popup. Toggle the heatmap or safe-zone overlays with the buttons on the lower left.

3

Plan an Evacuation Route

Switch to the Evacuate tab. Click Detect My Location, then type a destination city and press Calculate. A route appears on the map with distance, estimated time, and step-by-step directions. Download as PDF to use offline.

4

Set Up Alerts

In the Resources tab, set your alert radius and optionally add email or phone. Once your location is detected, you will see all events within that radius in the bell panel (top right).

5

Share "I'm Safe"

Tap the green button (bottom right of the map). Fill in your name and a brief message. Share the generated link with family or friends — it works for 24 hours.

Important disclaimer
EvacScan aggregates public news sources and open data. Information may be delayed, incomplete, or inaccurate. This tool supplements but does not replace official government guidance, military advisories, or emergency services. Always follow instructions from local authorities.
